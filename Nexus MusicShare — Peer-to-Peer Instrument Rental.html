Simple Process

How It Works

From curious beginner to playing your first note — it's just four steps.

🔍

Browse & Discover

Search instruments listed by experienced players in the Nexus community. Read their profiles and reviews.

💬

Chat & Connect

Send a rental request and chat with the lender. Ask questions, agree on dates and terms.

🤝

Meet & Pick Up

Coordinate a convenient pick-up at school. The lender shows you the basics — how to hold, play, and care for it.

🎵

Play & Return

Enjoy the instrument for your rental period! Return it on time and leave a review for the lender.

Why MusicShare

Built for the Nexus Community

We're not a rental company. We're a community of musicians helping each other out.

Real People, Real Instruments

Every instrument belongs to a fellow Nexus student. You know exactly who you're borrowing from — their skill level, experience, and reputation.

Learn from Experts

Not just an instrument — get tips and guidance from experienced players. Many lenders offer a quick intro lesson when you pick up.

Super Affordable

No company markup. Community-driven pricing means you pay a fraction of commercial rental rates. Perfect for trying before you buy.

Verified & Safe

All members verify with their Nexus school email. Damage deposit system protects both parties. Trust ratings keep everyone accountable.

In-App Messaging

Chat directly with lenders or borrowers to arrange pick-up, ask questions, or get advice. No need to exchange personal contact info.

School Meetup

All exchanges happen on campus — safe, convenient, and familiar. Pick up between classes, return after school. Simple.

FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about borrowing and lending on MusicShare.

MusicShare is exclusively for the Nexus International School community. All users must verify with a @nexus.edu email address. This ensures a trusted, safe environment for everyone.
A small damage deposit is held for each rental. For minor wear and tear, there's no charge. For significant damage, the deposit covers repairs up to a limit. Both parties agree on the instrument's condition at pick-up and return via photos in the app.
Lenders set their own prices, typically $15–40 per week — much cheaper than commercial rentals. MusicShare takes a small 8% service fee to maintain the platform. No hidden costs.
Many lenders offer a brief intro when you pick up — how to hold the instrument, basic care, and maybe your first note! It's not a formal lesson, but it's a great head start. Some lenders also mark themselves as "willing to mentor" on their profile.
All exchanges happen on the Nexus campus. You'll coordinate a time via in-app messaging — before school, during lunch, or after class. We recommend meeting in a public area like the music room or cafeteria.
Absolutely! If you have an instrument sitting idle — maybe you upgraded, or you only play it occasionally — list it on MusicShare. Set your own price and availability. It's a great way to help others and earn some extra pocket money.
